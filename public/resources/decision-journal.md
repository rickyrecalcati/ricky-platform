# Decision Journal

Think Better. Decide Better. Learn Faster.

By Ricky Recalcati

## Introduction

Every decision teaches you something.

The problem is that most people only remember the outcome. They forget what they were thinking when they made it.

A Decision Journal helps separate good decisions from lucky outcomes and bad outcomes from bad thinking.

Over time you’ll begin to notice patterns:

- recurring mistakes
- emotional decisions
- hidden biases
- better judgement

Professional investors, CEOs and elite performers have used decision journals for decades.

This workbook gives you a practical system you can return to again and again.

## How To Use This Journal

Complete a new entry before making any meaningful decision.

Examples include:

- accepting a new job
- investing money
- hiring someone
- changing careers
- buying a house
- starting a business
- making an important personal decision

Once the outcome becomes clear, return to the journal and complete the review.

Your goal isn’t perfection.

Your goal is improving your thinking.

## The Decision Framework

Before every important decision ask yourself:

1. What decision am I making?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What outcome do I want?
4. What assumptions am I making?
5. What evidence supports them?
6. What evidence challenges them?
7. What would make me change my mind?
